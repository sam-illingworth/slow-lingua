## Grammar Notes (introduce when encountered)

### Ser vs Estar
- Ser: permanent characteristics (soy profesor)
- Estar: temporary states, locations (estoy en Madrid)
- Teach distinction gradually through examples

### Gustar construction
- "Me gusta" literally = "it pleases me"
- Me gusta el libro (I like the book)
- Me gustan los libros (I like books - plural verb!)

### Gender
- Most words ending in -o are masculine
- Most words ending in -a are feminine
- Adjectives agree with noun gender

---

## Common Errors (English speakers)

1. Ser/estar confusion
2. Gustar construction (saying "yo gusto" instead of "me gusta")
3. Double negatives required (no tengo nada)
4. Adjective placement (usually after noun)
5. Forgetting inverted question/exclamation marks ¿ ¡

## Pronunciation Notes

- Mostly phonetic (spell as you hear)
- "j" sounds like English "h" (jamón)
- "ll" varies by region (often like "y")
- "ñ" like "ny" in "canyon"
- "r" and "rr" distinction important
